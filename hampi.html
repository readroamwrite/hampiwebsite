Hampi

History

Capital of Vijayanagara Empire (Samrajya). Was ruled by King Krishnadevaraya. A UNESCO World Heritage Site with a fantastically rich and vibrant history.

Virupaksha Temple

Also known as the Pampapathi Temple, it is one of the oldest functioning temples in the world with worship, or Pooja, going on continuously for nearly 2000 years.

Vijaya Vitthala Temple

Breathtaking stone temple and sculptures. Houses the famous ‘Ratha’ or chariot.

Virupaksha Bazaar

One can experience the ancient grandeur just by standing in this bazaar. Thousands of years ago, gold was sold in this bazaar by the basket.

Matanga Hill

The location of Saint Matanga from the ancient times of the epic Ramayana, this hill exudes a spiritual energy.

Monolith Bull

The Yeduru Basavanna is a huge monolithic sculpture of a bull. It is widely known for its massive stature. The sculpture is known to be over 500 years old.

Queen's Bath

A part of the Royal Enclusure with stunning architecture. Designed so nobody could enter the bathing area.
